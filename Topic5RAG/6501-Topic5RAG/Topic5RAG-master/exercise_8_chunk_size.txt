======================================================================
ANALYSIS
======================================================================

Key Findings on Chunk Size:

1. CHUNK_SIZE=128:
   - Very granular chunks
   - High retrieval precision (specific to query)
   - May lack sufficient context for complex answers
   - Large number of chunks to index (1,089)
   - Fast retrieval but answers may be incomplete

2. CHUNK_SIZE=256:
   - Good granularity
   - Better context than 128
   - Still may miss broader context
   - Moderate number of chunks (567)
   - Good for simple factual queries

3. CHUNK_SIZE=512 (RECOMMENDED):
   - Balanced granularity and context
   - Sufficient for most procedural questions
   - Good retrieval precision with adequate context
   - Reasonable index size (277 chunks)
   - Best trade-off for technical documents

4. CHUNK_SIZE=1024:
   - Larger context per chunk
   - More comprehensive answers
   - Lower precision (may include irrelevant text)
   - Fewer total chunks (134)
   - Risk of diluting relevance with extra content

5. OPTIMAL SIZE DEPENDS ON:
   - Document type (procedural vs narrative)
   - Query complexity
   - Required answer detail level
   - Corpus characteristics
   - Latency requirements

6. RETRIEVAL IMPLICATIONS:
   - Smaller chunks = more precise retrieval
   - Larger chunks = more context per retrieval
   - With top_k=5:
     - size=128: ~640 tokens context
     - size=512: ~2,560 tokens context
     - size=1024: ~5,120 tokens context
   - Larger context may exceed LLM window or dilute focus

======================================================================
RECOMMENDATIONS
======================================================================

DEFAULT: chunk_size=512 for technical/procedural documents
- Best balance of precision and context
- Fits most procedural answers completely
- Reasonable index size

USE SIZE=128-256 WHEN:
- Very specific factual lookups
- Large corpus (millions of documents)
- Latency is critical
- Questions are simple and discrete

USE SIZE=1024 WHEN:
- Long narrative documents
- Complex synthesis questions
- Small corpus where index size doesn't matter
- High context needed for understanding

AVOID EXTREMES:
- Too small (<128): Incomplete answers, excessive chunks
- Too large (>1024): Diluted relevance, may hit token limits

RULE OF THUMB:
- Technical manuals: 512-1024
- News articles: 256-512
- Code: 256-512
- Legal documents: 512-1024
- Chat logs: 128-256
