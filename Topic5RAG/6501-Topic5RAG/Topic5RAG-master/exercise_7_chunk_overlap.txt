======================================================================
ANALYSIS
======================================================================

Key Findings on Chunk Overlap:

1. OVERLAP=0:
   - No redundancy between chunks
   - Potential for information loss at chunk boundaries
   - Smallest index size
   - Risk of incomplete procedures that span boundaries

2. OVERLAP=64:
   - Minimal context preservation
   - Some continuity between adjacent chunks
   - Moderate index size increase
   - May still miss some boundary-spanning information

3. OVERLAP=128 (RECOMMENDED):
   - Good balance of context preservation and index size
   - Maintains continuity across chunk boundaries
   - Reasonable trade-off for most applications
   - Procedures are typically complete

4. OVERLAP=256:
   - High redundancy (50% overlap)
   - Maximum context preservation
   - Significantly larger chunks (more tokens per chunk)
   - Diminishing returns on answer quality
   - Higher embedding compute cost

5. IMPACT ON RETRIEVAL:
   - Higher overlap = more chances to retrieve complete information
   - But also more duplicate information in results
   - Sweet spot appears to be 64-128 for most corpora
   - Overlap >25% of chunk size shows diminishing returns

6. COST CONSIDERATIONS:
   - Higher overlap = fewer but larger chunks
   - Embedding cost is per-chunk, so fewer chunks = lower cost
   - But each chunk is processed multiple times (in overlaps)
   - Net effect: overlap 128 costs ~10% more than overlap 0

======================================================================
RECOMMENDATIONS
======================================================================

DEFAULT: chunk_overlap=128 with chunk_size=512
- Best balance for most applications
- Good continuity without excessive redundancy
- Reasonable index size

USE OVERLAP=64 WHEN:
- Corpus has short, discrete sections
- Memory/embedding cost is critical
- Content doesn't span boundaries often

USE OVERLAP=256 WHEN:
- Content has long, continuous narratives
- Maximum context preservation needed
- Cost is less important than completeness

AVOID OVERLAP=0:
- Too much risk of boundary truncation
- Only for very structured, short-section documents

RULE OF THUMB:
Overlap should be 12.5% to 25% of chunk size:
- chunk_size=512 → overlap=64 to 128
- chunk_size=1024 → overlap=128 to 256
